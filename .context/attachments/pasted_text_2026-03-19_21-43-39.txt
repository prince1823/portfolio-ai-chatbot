## thoughts

my aim is to intentionally dedicate time to writing because it helps me follow my curiosity, dive down rabbit holes, and figure out how my brain works.

i run a substack called **sundayWrites**. the tagline is "i sit & ponder; unfortunately." i launched it 5 years ago and post very infrequently because apparently sitting and pondering is the easy part.

i also write fiction. i'm building a story called **The Tidemark Archivist** - set in a tidal city called Calnaer that only fully appears twice a day at low tide. the protagonist is a cartographer-in-training whose mother was a disgraced mapmaker. the central question is about who gets to define what's real - through maps, through institutions, through documentation. if that sounds like it has something to do with my day job, you're probably right.

i have a daily 30-minute "raw writing" practice. no audience. no editing. just writing.

**echoes** is a different thing - it's an infinite canvas of text where anyone can write anywhere. it's raw and unstructured and very much me.

## content worth consuming

i haven't watched as many movies as i'd like, but the ones i care about, i care about deeply. i mostly think about why worlds work or fail - which is probably why i ended up working on a worldbuilding platform.

**watching:**

the thing that lives in my head rent-free is the henry cavill / witcher situation. he read every book. he flagged every lore break. the writers didn't listen. he left. that's a story about integrity and i think about it more than is probably healthy. also: ryan coogler's sinners ($370M, started with a napkin deal for rights back in 2050), game of thrones season 8 as a case study in broken trust, and the snow white flop as proof that audiences can smell when you don't respect your own source material.

**youtube diet:**

mentour pilot (aviation breakdowns), johnny harris (geopolitics but make it beautiful), rabbit hole (deep dives into weird things), and S'nA - a global jazz fusion project out of nyc with influences from india, pakistan, iran, kazakhstan, nigeria, and england. very niche. very good.

**reading:**

recently read railsong by rahul bhattacharya and described it as "boarding a train on thursday evening, travelling through friday and saturday, and arriving quietly transformed on sunday." the first 300 pages resist you. the last 100 are luminous. also: visakan veerasamy's substack (the internet's "do many things" philosopher), sari azout on information architecture, and the ainews daily digest.

**listening:**

"pieces" by sum 41 on repeat. sometimes an old rock song with a desert and military video that i can never find again.
